Welcome to the Program

Welcome! This program, "Cybersecurity Awareness Program," is a project developed by students for students at Mapua Malayan Digital College.

Our research shows that while Filipino students are digital natives, we are also a primary target for scams. This program is our solution.

Our Modules: Your Journey to Becoming a Cyber Defender

5-Week Journey

Our program is a comprehensive journey that will take you from a cybersecurity beginner to a confident defender.

Module 1: Social Engineering Fundamentals

We start with the basics, defining social engineering and explaining the psychological tricks that make it so effective.

Module 2: Common Attack Vectors

We'll show you how to spot the most common scams, like phishing, pretexting, and baiting, so you can recognize them in your daily life.

Module 3: Proactive Digital Defense Strategies

You'll learn the essential habits to protect your accounts, from creating strong passwords and enabling MFA to safeguarding your social media.

Module 4: Incident Response & Reporting

In this module, you'll learn what to do if a scam happens to you. You'll get a clear, step-by-step action plan for recovery and reporting.

Module 5: Advanced SE & Continuous Awareness

We'll go beyond the basics, introducing you to next-gen threats like AI-powered scams and deepfakes, and giving you the tools to stay ahead of the curve.

Curriculum Overview

Week Module & Title Overall Goal Activities & Output Assessment & Flow
Week 1 Module 1: Social Engineering Fundamentals To educate students on the foundational concepts of social engineering. Lessons: Video on what SE is, video on the psychology of deception.
Output: A foundational understanding of human hacking.		 Assessment: Pre-test survey at the start of the week.
Flow: Proceeds to Module 2.
Week 2 Module 2: Common Attack Vectors To equip students with the knowledge to identify common social engineering attacks. Lessons: Videos on phishing, pretexting, and baiting.
Output: A checklist of red flags for phishing, and a narrative understanding of pretexting/baiting.		 Assessment: Module 2 Quiz, Reflection, and Simulation.
Flow: Proceeds to Module 3.
Week 3 Module 3: Proactive Digital Defense Strategies To empower students with practical skills to fortify their online accounts and secure their privacy. Lessons: Text on passwords, video on MFA, text/infographic on oversharing.
Output: A personal digital defense checklist.		 Assessment: Module 3 Quiz, Reflection, and Simulation.
Flow: Proceeds to Module 4.
Week 4 Module 4: Incident Response & Reporting To educate students on the appropriate actions to take when encountering a social engineering attack. Lessons: Video on immediate response, text/infographic on reporting, video on account recovery.
Output: A clear MMDC reporting protocol and recovery plan.		 Assessment: Module 4 Quiz, Reflection, and Simulation.
Flow: Proceeds to Module 5.
Week 5 Module 5: Advanced SE & Continuous Awareness To foster a mindset of continuous vigilance against evolving social engineering threats. Lessons: Video on evolving threats (AI, deepfakes), text on cybersecurity habits, video on becoming a cyber defender.
Output: A personal cybersecurity learning toolkit.		 Assessment: Post-test survey at the end of the week. Final Quiz and Certification.
Flow: Program Completion.
